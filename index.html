WINEMAKER & ESTATE STORIES
Adami has farmed the Valdobbiadene hillsides since 1920 — four generations deep in the Prosecco heartland. Their Bosco di Gica is a single-vineyard wine from vines over 40 years old, rare in a region dominated by volume production. Cleto Chiarli was founded in 1860 in Modena and is the oldest Lambrusco producer in the region — they essentially defined the style the world came to know. Laurent-Perrier was founded in 1812 and remained family-controlled under the de Nonancourt family for generations. Their Cuvée Rosé, introduced in 1968, pioneered the rosé Champagne category using skin maceration of Pinot Noir only — no blending of red wine, which sets it apart technically and historically. Grand Siècle blends multiple exceptional vintages; Bernard de Nonancourt called it 'recreating the perfect vintage.' Triennes was founded in 1989 by two Burgundy legends: Jacques Seysses of Domaine Dujac and Aubert de Villaine of Domaine de la Romanée-Conti. They chose Provence deliberately — wanting to explore Mediterranean terroir after lifetimes in Burgundy. For $66 a bottle, the Romanée-Conti connection makes it one of the great value stories on the entire list. Wolfer Estate uses vacuum distillation to remove alcohol at low temperatures, specifically to preserve the aromatic compounds that heat-based methods would destroy — it is a technically serious wine designed to function at the table, not a grape juice substitute.
1

Adami's Bosco di Gica is a single-vineyard Prosecco DOCG from 40-year-old vines on the Valdobbiadene hillsides — a terroir-specific approach uncommon in a region dominated by large-scale volume production.

2

Cleto Chiarli is the oldest Lambrusco producer in Modena, founded in 1860 — their version is completely dry and food-forward, the opposite of the sweet Lambrusco that flooded the American market in the 1970s.

3

4

5

6

Wolfer Estate uses vacuum distillation to preserve aromatics without heat — it functions as a genuine wine experience at the table and is the most important tool for including non-drinking guests in a wine pairing moment.

WINEMAKER & ESTATE STORIES
CADE Estate sits on Howell Mountain in Napa at 1,800 feet — owned by PlumpJack Group, the same family behind PlumpJack Winery and a network of Bay Area hospitality. Mountain-grown Sauvignon Blanc is rare in Napa; volcanic soils and elevation give it a minerality that valley-floor versions cannot replicate. Illumination is the white wine from Quintessa, Napa's renowned single-estate red blend. Quintessa was founded by Agustin and Valeria Huneeus in 1990 on 280 acres in Rutherford — biodynamically farmed, every acre managed as a single organism. Illumination is the white expression of that philosophy. Knights Bridge is a 35-acre Sonoma estate focused almost entirely on single-vineyard whites — a boutique scale rare in California. Sager & Verdier is a Loire négociant producing Sancerre from Kimmeridgian chalk — the same ancient seabed soils that define great Chablis — giving the wine its signature flinty, saline mineral edge.
1

CADE's Howell Mountain location at 1,800 feet means cooler growing conditions despite Napa's warm reputation — volcanic soils and elevation produce Sauvignon Blanc with tension, brightness, and minerality uncommon on the valley floor.

4

WINEMAKER & ESTATE STORIES
Morgan Winery was founded in 1982 by Dan and Donna Lee in Carmel Valley — they were among the first to recognize Santa Lucia Highlands as a serious Chardonnay region, where cold Pacific fog and high elevation produce natural acidity rare in California. The Prisoner Wine Company was created by Dave Phinney in 2000 and sold for $285 million in 2010 after becoming one of California's most recognizable labels; their Chardonnay follows the same philosophy as their red — rich, generous, and broadly appealing. DuMOL was founded in 1996 by two Australian partners, Andy Smith and Roy CaBilan, as a small-production Sonoma project; 'Chloe' is named for a founder's daughter and has become one of California's most celebrated cool-climate Chardonnays. Domaine Ferret in Pouilly-Fuissé was run for decades by Colette Ferret-Lorton — a legendary Burgundy figure who produced wines of unusual depth before Louis Jadot acquired the estate in 2008 while preserving its identity and approach.
1

Morgan's Santa Lucia Highlands location at high elevation with Pacific fog influence produces Chardonnay with natural acidity uncommon in California — it is a genuinely cool-climate wine at a by-the-glass price point.

2

Dave Phinney built The Prisoner into a $285M brand on the principle of rich, approachable, crowd-pleasing wine — their Chardonnay is intentionally riper and creamier, exactly right for guests who say they like buttery Chardonnay.

3

DuMOL 'Chloe' from Russian River Valley is small-production, critically acclaimed, and one of California's benchmark cool-climate Chardonnays — the price reflects scarcity and quality, not marketing.

4

Domaine Ferret Pouilly-Fuissé represents the Burgundian tradition: mineral-driven, restrained oak, food-first — Colette Ferret-Lorton's philosophy of letting Chardonnay speak for its limestone terroir rather than winemaking intervention.
